About One thread runs through everything I've done.

I'm a registered nurse at Stanford, where I began my career in inpatient psychiatry as a new graduate nurse and have worked ever since. Alongside nursing, I've worked in the MedSpa industry performing laser hair removal and aesthetic treatments. More recently, I've been expanding my focus toward functional medicine and wellness, exploring ways to help people feel healthier from the inside out.

Before nursing, I was a licensed massage therapist and spent several years as a brand specialist for contemporary and luxury fashion houses at Neiman Marcus and Saks.

On paper, those careers may seem unrelated, but to me they've always been the same work. Every role has centered on understanding people. Whether I was recognizing a client's preferences, creating a treatment plan, or caring for a patient in crisis, the goal was the same: understand what someone truly needs, often beyond what they're able to express, and turn that understanding into meaningful care.

Outside of work, I stay intentionally active through strength training, HIIT, long-distance running, yoga, and meditation. I'm equally interested in nutrition, recovery, and the daily habits that support physical and mental well-being. These practices aren't hobbies to me; they're part of how I stay grounded and aligned with the way I want to live.

What fascinates me most is human behavior. I've always wanted to understand why people become who they are, what shapes their choices, and how they find resilience through life's challenges. That curiosity is what drew me to healthcare, wellness, and the study of human behavior in the first place. It's also how I connect with people. The more I understand someone's experiences, the more empathy I have for them.

I learn through observation as much as conversation. I enjoy paying attention to what people do, not just what they say, and I've found that understanding others has also helped me better understand myself. That perspective has shaped both my personal life and the way I care for others.

I've always considered myself an observer first. I love immersing myself in an environment and absorbing what's happening emotionally, behaviorally, and socially. That's why product research feels like such a natural fit. It combines curiosity, empathy, and careful observation to create experiences that genuinely improve people's lives at scale.

How I work

Research in everything but name.

01

Observe before assuming

I walk in and pay attention first, noticing the small details about a person that tell me who they are before I ask a single question.

02

Let people teach me

I ask open, direct questions and let people tell me what they actually need, rather than deciding for them. The truest insights live in guarded moments.

03

Turn signal into action

Every detail is a data point. I synthesize messy human signals into clear recommendations a team can act on, the same way every nursing handoff works.
